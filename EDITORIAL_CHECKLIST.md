# AdvancedAI Editorial Checklist

Use this before any article moves from draft to live content.

## Universal Gates

- [ ] The piece fits the About-page promise: disciplined curiosity for business operators.
- [ ] The tier is correct: briefing vs analysis.
- [ ] Every factual claim with numbers, named companies, dates, or attributed statements has a source link.
- [ ] Primary sources are used where available: company posts, SEC filings, earnings calls, product docs, research papers.
- [ ] Vendor-funded claims are caveated or de-emphasized.
- [ ] The article distinguishes announcement, pilot, deployment, and actual business impact.
- [ ] The piece is skeptical of vendor framing without becoming cynical.
- [ ] No unsupported prediction or hype language.
- [ ] The article includes a practical operator takeaway or decision point.
- [ ] The conclusion adds judgment; it does not merely summarize.

## Briefing Gates

Target: fast, sourced, business-framed coverage.

- [ ] 400–700 words target; above 800 requires explicit justification.
- [ ] 3–5 short body sections maximum.
- [ ] 2–3 FAQs maximum.
- [ ] At least 2 external sources, preferably primary or tier-1 reporting.
- [ ] Lede answers what happened and why it matters in the first 80 words.
- [ ] Includes one sharp observation that would not appear in a generic news recap.
- [ ] Ends with what to watch next, not a long-range prediction.

## Analysis Gates

Target: slower synthesis and judgment on structural shifts.

- [ ] 1,200–2,500 words; length is earned by synthesis, not padding.
- [ ] One-sentence original thesis is clear before drafting.
- [ ] Passes at least two: six-month test, synthesis test, depth test, originality test.
- [ ] Key takeaways block near the top.
- [ ] At least 5 external sources, including independent sources where possible.
- [ ] Strongest independent source appears in the first third.
- [ ] Has at least one real, named, attributable case per major claim.
- [ ] Includes a real counterpoint that makes the thesis feel at risk for a paragraph.
- [ ] Answers the operator sequencing question: what should the reader do this quarter?
- [ ] Names any original framework or taxonomy.
- [ ] FAQ answers add value and do not simply restate body copy.

## Approval Labels

- `APPROVED`: ready to move into live folder.
- `REVISE`: specific fixes required.
- `KILL`: do not publish.
- `HOLD_FOR_ANALYSIS`: strong idea, wrong format/timing.
- `NEEDS_EROL`: business/editorial approval required.
